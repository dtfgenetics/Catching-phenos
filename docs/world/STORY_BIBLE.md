# Story Bible

# PhenoQuest: The Living Seed Vault

## Core Premise

Verdantia is a fantasy world where living genetic expressions called Phenos are connected to an ancient archive known as the Living Seed Vault.

Each Pheno carries a Genetic Echo. When the Vault is healthy, wild Phenos express many forms, traits, and lineages. When the Vault is damaged, expressions become unstable and rare lineages begin to disappear.

Team Lockout is trying to control the Vault by replacing living variation with sterile synthetic lines. The player becomes a Vault Runner and works with Professor Calyx to restore the Vault before Verdantia loses its living diversity.

## Main Theme

**Living variation beats sterile control.**

## Player Role

The player is a new Vault Runner from Seedling Town.

Their mission is to:

1. Explore Verdantia.
2. Battle wild Phenos for game material.
3. Build a team through the Vault Garden systems.
4. Find Keepers.
5. Study genotype markers.
6. Make lineages through the Lineage Lab.
7. Restore Vault Branches.
8. Defeat Team Lockout.

## Story Acts

### Act 1 — The Vault Flickers

Professor Calyx calls the player to the Greenhouse after the Living Seed Vault begins losing power. The player chooses a starter Pheno from old Vault stock. Team Lockout attacks and steals a Vault Tag.

### Act 2 — Terp Fields

The player follows Team Lockout into Terp Fields. Wild Phenos are behaving strangely because a Signal Clamp is blocking normal expression. The player learns the first battle, PhenoLog, and Vault Garden systems.

### Act 3 — The First Garden Seal

The player enters Aroma Trial, identifies expression clues, defeats Aroma Keeper Luma, earns the Terp Seal, and restores the Fruit Branch of the Vault.

### Act 4 — Roots of the Problem

In Kushwood Forest, the player learns that stable common lineages are just as important as rare power lines. Nova begins chasing strength and rarity too aggressively.

### Act 5 — Power Without Stability

Gas Cave reveals Team Lockout's extraction machines and unstable synthetic Phenos. The player learns that raw power without Stability damages lineages.

### Act 6 — The Synthetic City

Clone District is a sterile city controlled by Team Lockout. Nova is tricked into trusting synthetic power and must be rescued after a synthetic Pheno destabilizes.

### Act 7 — The Living Seed Vault

Team Lockout attempts to overwrite the Vault with the Null Cultivar. The player's restored lineages weaken the Null Cultivar and awaken Legacy Seed.

## Main Characters

### Professor Calyx

The player's mentor. Former Vault Runner. Knows more about the Vault than anyone but carries guilt from failing to stop Team Lockout years ago.

### Nova

The rival. Competitive, talented, and impatient. Believes strength and rarity matter most. Learns that Stability, lineage, and preservation matter more than raw power.

### Director Voss

Leader of Team Lockout. Believes wild variation is messy and should be replaced by controlled, registered synthetic lines.

### Grunt Pike

First recurring Team Lockout enemy. Steals the first Vault Tag and introduces Lockout tactics.

### Aroma Keeper Luma

First Garden Trial Keeper. Teaches the player to read expression clues instead of relying only on battle strength.

## Final Boss Concept

### Null Cultivar

A synthetic boss created by Team Lockout to overwrite the Living Seed Vault. It has no natural Echo, no true lineage, and no stable expression. It is powerful but empty.

## Legendary Concept

### Legacy Seed

A dormant Vault entity that grows stronger as the player restores lineages. Its final form depends on how much of the PhenoLog, Keeper system, and lineage archive the player completes.
